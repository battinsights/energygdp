IEA Energy Statistics (TES & TFC) · World Bank GDP PPP (constant 2021 int. $) · Bubble size = population

Unweighted fit   Population-weighted fit
The fitted curve follows a power law: E = a · GDPβ, where E is energy (EJ), GDP is in trillions of constant 2021 int. $, a is the scale coefficient (energy use when GDP = 1 trillion), and β (the exponent) is the GDP–energy elasticity.
β — a 1 % increase in GDP is associated with a β % increase in energy use. β < 1 means energy grows slower than GDP (efficiency gains at higher income); β > 1 means energy-intensive growth. The pop-weighted variant gives larger countries proportionally more influence on the fit.
R² — fraction of variance in log(Energy) explained by log(GDP); 1.0 = perfect fit.   r — Pearson correlation between log(GDP) and log(Energy); values near ±1 indicate a tight relationship.
